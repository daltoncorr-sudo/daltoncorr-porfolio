# Copy & Voice Guide
## How everything reads on the new site

---

## D'S VOICE

Direct. Confident. Zero filler. Warm but not soft. Says it once, says it well, moves on.

Think: the way a sharp creative talks to another creative they respect. Not pitching. Not performing. Just clear.

**Not this:** "Dalton Corr is a multi-disciplinary creative professional specializing in the intersection of visual design and musical composition, with a proven track record of delivering elevated brand experiences for entertainment industry clients."

**This:** "I design the visual identity of film festivals and score the films that play in them. Based in Los Angeles."

## ABOUT PAGE — BIO

### Lead paragraph (this is the only thing most people will read):

```
Dalton Corr is a creative director, designer, and composer based in Los Angeles.

He is the art director for the Oscar-qualifying HollyShorts Film Festival, where 
he's led creative direction across four consecutive years — designing everything 
from the festival poster and production design at TCL Chinese Theatres to brand 
identity systems for HollyShorts London and HollyShorts Comedy.

His design work spans film festival art direction, theatrical poster design, and 
brand identity — with projects at the Cannes Film Festival for Kodak, commercial 
production for Weissman, and rebranding for HDtracks. His music work includes 
feature-length film scores, award-winning short film compositions, and original 
releases on Bastard Jazz Recordings.

He designs the worlds that films live in, and writes the music that lives in them.
```

D should rewrite this in his own words. The above is a structural template — the bones are right, the voice should be his.

### Supporting details (smaller text, below the bio):

```
Education: New York University — B.M. Music Composition & Music Business
Cited in: Lennon and McCartney: Painting With Sound
Press: Variety, Deadline, Daily Mail, Canvas Rebel, NOWNESS, Radio du Cinéma
```

Keep it sparse. If Espresso Tempo / Corr Tunes are active, add a line. If dormant, leave them out.

## PROJECT PAGE COPY — WRITING GUIDELINES

### Tone per section:

**Title:** Just the name. No subtitle, no tagline.
- ✓ "21st Annual HollyShorts Film Festival"
- ✗ "21st Annual HollyShorts Film Festival — A Celebration of Short Film"

**Details line:** Facts only. Role — Client — Location — Year.
- ✓ "Art Director — HollyShorts Film Festival — Los Angeles — 2025"
- ✗ "Served as Art Director for the prestigious HollyShorts Film Festival"

**Body text:** This is where the voice comes through. Write like D is explaining the project to someone he wants to work with. Not a case study brief. Not marketing copy. A clear, confident description of what the work was and why it mattered.

### Body text formulas:

**For art direction / design projects:**
```
[One sentence: what is this project.]
[One sentence: D's specific role and scope.]
[Two-three sentences: what D actually made — deliverables in flowing prose, 
not bullet points. Include venues, scale, anything that shows scope.]
[One sentence: outcome, press, or impact — if applicable.]
```

**Example (HollyShorts 20th):**
```
Art directed the 20th anniversary of the Oscar-qualifying HollyShorts Film 
Festival across three Los Angeles venues — TCL Chinese Theatres, the 
Egyptian Theater, and Japan House.

Work included the official anniversary poster (original illustration), 
a full merchandise line (hoodies, shirts, hats, totes), ten unique badge 
designs, the Revello award design, and complete production design for the 
festival, the Alta Global Media Film Summit, and the award show at the 
Egyptian Theater. From step-and-repeats and lobby installations to digital 
billboards and on-screen graphics — every visual touchpoint of the festival.

Featured in Variety, Deadline, Daily Mail, and named one of the "world's 
greatest film festival posters" by FestivalsInLA.
```

**For film scores:**
```
[One sentence: what is this film.]
[One sentence: D's role — composer, music supervisor, etc.]
[One-two sentences: the music itself — tone, instrumentation, anything 
distinctive about the score.]
[One sentence: festivals, awards, press — if applicable.]
```

**Example (Alchemy):**
```
Composed the score and served as music supervisor for Alchemy, a documentary 
about Bonnie Ohara, founder of a one-woman bakery in Modesto, California. 
Directed by Remington Long.

Winner of over ten awards including selections at the American Documentary 
and Animation Festival, Greenwich Village Film Festival (Best Documentary), 
Napa Valley Film Festival, Hamptons Film Festival, and Sundance Collab.
```

### Words to use:
- Art directed, designed, composed, scored, led, built, created
- Visual identity, brand system, production design, creative direction
- Original illustration, original score, original composition

### Words to avoid:
- Synergy, elevated, curated, bespoke, holistic, innovative
- Passion, passionate, passionate about
- Multi-disciplinary (show it, don't say it)
- Proud to, excited to, honored to
- "Had the opportunity to" — just say what you did

## DISCIPLINE TAG LABELS

Use these exact labels on project cards and project pages. Uppercase, consistent everywhere.

| Tag | When to use |
|-----|-------------|
| ART DIRECTION | D led the overall visual direction of a project |
| DESIGN | General graphic design — posters, merch, collateral |
| BRANDING | Logo, identity system, brand guidelines |
| PRODUCTION DESIGN | Physical event/space design — lobbies, signage, step-and-repeats |
| ILLUSTRATION | Original hand-made artwork or illustration |
| WEB DESIGN | Website design or redesign |
| FILM SCORE | Composed music for a film |
| ORIGINAL SONG | Wrote/produced an original song (not for a film) |
| LIVE PERFORMANCE | Live music performance |

Never use: `MUSIC` as a standalone tag (too vague — use FILM SCORE, ORIGINAL SONG, or LIVE PERFORMANCE). Never use `ART` alone.

## PRESS PAGE — ENTRY FORMAT

Each press entry follows this pattern:

```
[Outlet Name] — [Article Title or Description]    [→ link]
```

If the article title is unknown, use a brief descriptor:
```
Variety — HollyShorts Film Festival 2024 Coverage
```

If the article title is known:
```
Canvas Rebel — "Meet Dalton Corr"
```

Group by year, newest first. Year headers are simple:

```
2024
────────────────────────────────────
Variety — HollyShorts Film Festival 2024 Coverage
Deadline — HollyShorts 20th Anniversary
...

2023
────────────────────────────────────
...
```

## NAVIGATION LABELS

```

```

Uppercase. That's it. No "Portfolio" (too generic). No "Music" or "Design" as separate tabs (unified under Work). No "Contact" (it's on the About page).
